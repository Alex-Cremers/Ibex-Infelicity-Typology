Hello, and thank you for your participation!


Task: In this experiment, you will read a series of sentences. Your task will be to judge whether each sentence sounds like a natural English sentence, or on the contrary, if you think only a non-native speaker could have said it. We are not interested in whether the sentences are true or false, only whether they sound natural to you. You will answer the question “Does the sentence sound natural?” by adjusting a slider between “definitely not” and “definitely”.


Example 1

“Montana has the largest population among all US states.”

Does the sentence sound natural?

In this example, you would give a positive answer. Even if the sentence is clearly false, it is well-formed English, and that's all that matters here.

Example 2

“California population largest US state.”

Does the sentence sound natural?

In this example, you would give a negative answer, because the sentence is clearly ill-formed, even though its likely intended meaning is true.


Before you start:

  • You will give your responses using a slider. The slider follows your cursor and clicking validates your answer.
  • At the end of the survey, after submitting your responses, you will receive a code to copy into Prolific to validate your participation.
  • The survey should take 5 to 10 min.
  • Don't forget that we do not care about the meaning of the sentences, only whether they sound natural in English.
